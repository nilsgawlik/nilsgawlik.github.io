Hi!

The fact that you received this mail means that we talked at A Maze and you signed up for the "Indie School Trip" Mailing List, or you signed up through the link in the Indie Arena Discord.

This is the first Mail and I want to summarize the status of the project and what the future will hold.

All in all we are a little bit more that 20 people, and some of you were pretty excited about this project. The idea went through a few iterations already, but this is the essence of it:

We want to organize a small "school trip" for indie games developers and university students. For this we will go to a vacation spot (cabin, hostel, camping, etc.) and have a game jam. On top of that we will also do activities like campfire, bread on a stick, having a night walk, whatever feels appropriate.

During the last two years I have regularly been to game jams and one thing I noticed is that often there will be little exchange outside of the teams. 95% of people will work like maniacs on their games and then pack their things at the end of the jam and go home. This is where we want to improve things and organize a game jam where social interaction (eeewhh! D:) is the focus. To achieve this we will create an environment and activities that encourage people to come together and have a talk.

The whole concept was originally conceived at the Indie Arena Meetup and I volunteered to do the organization.

At the moment the are two goals: One proof of concept event this September (less people, relatively little work, no sponsors, financed by the participants) and one bigger event next year for which we can get sponsors and offer more stuff for a cheaper price.

The reason for this is that there is not enough time this summer to organize something bigger, but if this year goes well, we will expand! The remainder of this mail I will be talking about the event this summer.
